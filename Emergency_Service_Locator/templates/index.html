Algorithm Insights

The Divide & Conquer approach recursively splits the plane in half. The closest pair is either entirely in the left half, entirely in the right half, or split across the mid-line (the strip). We only need to check points within the strip that are closer than the current minimum distance.

Theory & Complexity

Divide & Conquer Strategy

The problem is to find the closest pair of points in a set of N points. By sorting the points according to their X-coordinates, we can split them into two equal halves. We recursively find the smallest distance in both halves.

T(n) = 2T(n/2) + O(n)

Where O(n) is the time taken to check the "strip" area.
